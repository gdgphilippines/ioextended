I/O Extended Roadshow

GDG Philippines will bring the I/O experience to different cities and help the professional and student developers deep dive in the new technologies. The Google I/O Extended Roadshow is a series of workshops and talks centered around the technologies announced during the Google I/O 2016. We'll have an amazing roster of speakers Googlers, Googler Developer Experts and more to come so stay tuned.

Last year's Roadshow

Last year’s roadshow was held in 6 cities around the country: Pampanga, Manila, Batangas, Albay, Camarines Sur and Cavite. In each roadshow, participants experienced what it felt like being in the actual I/O Conference in California, USA. Participants were given the opportunity to see what’s in store for Google with the presentation from last year’s Keynote, aside from talks on other Google technologies and from speakers specifically chosen for this roadshow.

Aside from the talks, Code Labs were given in the afternoon. Participants were delighted to be able to learn how Google apps and products are made using different technologies including Android, HTML5, Polymer and many more.

Of course I/O Extended will never be complete without the freebies the participants received and the cool booths that were set up to enhance the I/O experience.

This year, Google Developers Group PH brings back the much-awaited roadshow to let more people have the I/O experience. See you there.



Live Viewing

Google I/O is Google's annual developer conference, where they share the latest products and technologies. Google I/O 2016 will be held in Mountain View on May 18-20. If you are not attending in person, you can get the I/O experience by watching I/O Live at the website or by attending Google I/O Extended events being organized by Google Developer Groups and other communities worldwide.

GDG Philippines is organizing I/O Extended Manila Live-Viewing Party which will be held at Globe Corporate Showroom, Valero Telepark, No. 111 Valero Street, Makati City. (Map: http://bit.ly/Map-ValeroTelepark) on May 18-19 (08:00 PM of May 18 to 05:00 AM of May 19).

Unfortunately, the registration is closed. Although, you may still watch the live streaming of Google I/O here.
